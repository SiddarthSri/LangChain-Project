# Ooty — The Queen of Hill Stations

## Overview
- **Full Name**: Udhagamandalam (officially), popularly known as Ooty
- **Location**: Nilgiris District, Tamil Nadu, India
- **Altitude**: ~2,240 metres (7,350 ft) above sea level
- **Nickname**: The Queen of Hill Stations
- **Nearest City**: Coimbatore (~88 km away)

## Geography
- Situated in the Nilgiri Hills (Blue Mountains) of the Western Ghats
- Surrounded by eucalyptus forests, tea plantations, and grasslands
- The Nilgiris Biosphere Reserve covers much of the surrounding area
- Key water body: Ooty Lake (man-made, built in 1825)

## Climate
- **Summer (Mar–Jun)**: Cool and pleasant, 15°C–25°C — best time to visit
- **Monsoon (Jul–Sep)**: Heavy rainfall, lush greenery
- **Winter (Oct–Feb)**: Cold, often dropping to 0°C–5°C at night
- No extreme heat; the elevation keeps temperatures naturally cool year-round

## History
- Originally home to the Toda tribe, who still live in the region
- Developed as a hill station by the British East India Company in the early 1800s
- John Sullivan (Collector of Coimbatore) is credited with "founding" modern Ooty in 1821
- Served as the summer capital of the Madras Presidency during British rule

## Famous Attractions
- **Ooty Lake**: Scenic boating lake surrounded by eucalyptus trees
- **Botanical Gardens (Government Rose Garden)**: One of the finest botanical gardens in India, established 1848
- **Doddabetta Peak**: Highest peak in the Nilgiris at 2,637 m; panoramic views
- **Nilgiri Mountain Railway (Toy Train)**: UNESCO World Heritage Site — a steam-powered narrow-gauge railway from Mettupalayam to Ooty
- **Pykara Lake & Falls**: Peaceful lake and waterfall ~19 km from Ooty
- **Mudumalai National Park**: Tiger reserve ~67 km away; rich in wildlife

## Culture & People
- Home to the indigenous **Toda tribe**, known for their unique barrel-shaped huts and embroidery
- Mix of Tamil, Kannada, and Badaga communities
- Local festivals: Ooty Flower Show (May), Summer Festival (May), and Pongal

## Food & Local Specialties
- **Ooty Homemade Chocolate**: Famous across India; sold everywhere in town
- **Varkey** (local sweet bread): A traditional Nilgiri baked snack
- **Fresh Tea**: Nilgiri tea is world-renowned for its brisk, fragrant flavour
- **Ooty Jam & Preserve**: Strawberry and other fruit preserves
- **Carrot Halwa**: Locally grown carrots are sweeter at this altitude

## Economy
- **Tea Industry**: Major economic driver; Nilgiri tea estates cover thousands of hectares
- **Tourism**: Millions of domestic tourists visit annually, especially May–June
- **Agriculture**: Carrots, potatoes, cabbage, and other vegetables thrive in the cool climate
- **Eucalyptus Oil**: Produced from the extensive eucalyptus forests

## How to Get There
- **By Train**: Nilgiri Mountain Railway from Mettupalayam (UNESCO heritage toy train)
- **By Road**: Well-connected via NH-181; ~5 hrs from Chennai, ~3.5 hrs from Bangalore
- **By Air**: Nearest airport is Coimbatore International Airport (~88 km)

## Fun Facts
- Ooty was featured in numerous Bollywood and Tamil films
- The word "Nilgiri" means "Blue Mountains" in Tamil — named for the purple-blue Kurinji flowers that bloom once every 12 years
- The toy train covers 46 km and passes through 16 tunnels and 250 bridges
- Ooty chocolate is not factory-made — most shops hand-craft it using local recipes
